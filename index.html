What is GRID?

GRID (Governance Risk and Infrastructure Diagnostics for SEND-AI Environments) is a computational governance framework for non-verbal Special Educational Needs and Disabilities settings.

It addresses a structural gap. Non-verbal autistic learners cannot self-report safeguarding concerns. Their behaviour is the only disclosure mechanism available. But behaviour that staff observe and recognise as a pre-crisis signal frequently does not reach the institutional record, because narrative documentation under high workload selects for crisis-level incidents and filters out the ambiguous and the sub-threshold.

What this Observatory shows

You are watching one classroom in a SEND school across a working week. The Focal Student is a non-verbal learner with a documented escalation history. Trained staff and untrained staff move around the room observing learners. Above them in the governance chain sit the Behavioural Lead and the Designated Safeguarding Lead, each in their own office.

Three governance conditions can be selected. Standard Baseline uses retrospective narrative reporting only. Naive AI watches for unclassified concern volume and fires a generalised room-wide threat alert when it crosses a threshold. GRID presents staff with a structured CPI-aligned input, aggregates patterns across the floor, and routes targeted flags up the chain: cumulative harm signals to the DSL, environmental and sensory trigger signals to the Behavioural Lead.

Why the building view matters

The governance chain is rendered spatially. When a flag is routed under GRID, you see the signal travel from the classroom down the corridor to the correct office. Under Naive AI, the alert never leaves the room. Under Baseline, nothing structured leaves at all. The architecture difference is visible.

What am I seeing?

Green halo · Regulated stage. The learner is calm.
Amber halo · Anxiety stage. Early sub-crisis signal.
Orange halo · Defensive stage. Escalation in progress.
Red halo · Risk Behaviour. Crisis-level behaviour.
Teal centre · CPI-trained staff member.
White centre · Untrained or supply staff.
Cyan beam · An observation event. A staff member is noticing a learner.
Teal pulse along corridor · A CPI-classified flag routing to the DSL or Behavioural Lead under GRID.
Orange pulse from edge · The external trigger event (Thursday precipitant or manual trigger).
Red room wash · Naive AI threat alert. Staff defensive stance jumps room-wide.
